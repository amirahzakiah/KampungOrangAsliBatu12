CULTURE & HERITAGE


SEWANG DANCE

Sewang Sambutan

  • Sewang is also more intended as a fun event to welcome guests. Presented to the guests is as a sign of welcome to the guests.
  • This dance tradition is also performed during wedding ceremonies. This provides an opportunity for the bride's family to have fun and socialize after a tiring day.
  • This dance is able to last until late at night until they are tired and satisfied.

MUSICAL INSTRUMENT

Chentong Bamboo

  • This musical instruments produce an echoing sound through the beat of a bamboo determined by the size of the bamboo. This musical instrument is also used extensively by the Indigenous People community as the main music.

Nose Flute (Pensol)

  • A musical instrument of the Temiar tribe that produces sound using the nose.

MARRIAGE

  • Most Indigenous People marry at a young age.
  • The intention to get married will be informed to the Tok Batin/Village Chief first, and then to the parents of both couples.
  • Once all parties agreed, the preparations for the wedding ceremony must be made within 2 to 3 months from the date of the agreement.
  • The wedding couch is made of woven coconut leaves or numbia, while the pillars are made of small parts of wooden designed like a mini house. It is made in front of the bride's house.
  • If one tribe inhabits a long house, they will stand side by side in the middle of the space to be seen by other family members.

HANDCRAFTS

Simpai Bracelet

  • It is a women's jewelry accessory for the Temiar indigenous tribe.

Tempok

  • Woven accessories worn on the head of the Temiar tribe.
  • This tempok is worn by the Indigenous People according to the ceremonies held.
  • Versions and variations of the Tempok varies according to the tribes of the natives.

HUNTING TOOLS

Sumpit

  • Sumpit is a common hunting equipment of the Indigenous People community.
  • The hunting weapons made of bamboo along with poisonous darts are blown through bamboo holes to determine targets while hunting.
  • This hunting tool is also one of the identity icons for the Indigenous People community to the public.

Beliung

  • A hunting weapon that resembles an axe is used for butchering and cutting wood.

Bubu

  • Tool used as a trap to catch fish.
